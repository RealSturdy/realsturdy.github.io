My Research

Development of a backpack model for musculoskeletal simulations

Modeling the interaction between a backpack and the human body as a combination of linear springs.

https://doi.org/10.1016/j.apergo.2020.103277

We developed a backpack model that distributes the weight of a backpack between the shoulder straps and hip belt. Using self-selected walking data, we developed load carriage simulations to quantify forces in the lumbar spine under different load distributions. This paper demonstrates a novel approach to modeling the force interaction between a backpack and the wearer, which can be applied to many different walking conditions to better understand how load distribution influences injury risk.
